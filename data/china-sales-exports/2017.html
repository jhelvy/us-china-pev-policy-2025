China vehicle sales in 2017 reach 28,879,000 units, up 3% y/y
On January 11, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced vehicle production and sales results for 2017.
Production totaled approximately 29,015,000 units, a 3.2% increase on a year-over-year (y/y) basis, while sales totaled 28,879,000 units, a 3% increase y/y. These growth rates were 11.3 and 10.6 percentage points lower than those in 2016, respectively.
Passenger vehicles: Production reached 24,807,000 units, a 1.6% increase y/y; while sales reached 24,718,000 units, a 1.4% increase y/y. The y/y growth rates were both 1.6 percentage points lower than those for the overall market. Production of passenger vehicles accounts for 85.5% of the total production, while sales account for 85.6%. These proportions were 1.3 and 1.4 percentage points lower than those in 2016, respectively.
Commercial vehicles: Production reached 4,209,000 units, up 13.8% y/y; while sales reached 4,161,000 units, up 14% y/y. These growth rates were 5.8 and 8.2 percentage points higher than those in 2016, respectively.
New energy vehicles: Production totaled 794,000 units, a 53.8% increase y/y; while sales totaled 777,000 units, a 53.3% increase y/y. These growth rates were 2.1 and 0.3 percentage points higher than those in 2016, respectively. Production of electric passenger vehicles reached 478,000 units, up 81.7% y/y, while EV sales reached 468,000 units, up 82.1% y/y. Production of plug-in hybrid passenger vehicles reached 114,000 units, up 40.3% y/y, while PHV sales reached 111,000 units, up 39.4% y/y. Production of commercial EVs reached 188,000 units, up 22.2% y/y, while sales reached 184,000 units, up 21.5% y/y. Production of commercial PHVs reached 14,000 units, a 24.9% y/y decline, while sales reached 14,000 units, a 26.6% decline.
Exports from China totaled 891,000 vehicles, a sharp increase of 25.8% y/y, with 639,000 passenger vehicles (up 34% y/y) and 252,000 commercial vehicles (down 8.9% y/y) shipped overseas. This is the first increase after four years of declining exports.
CAAM forecasts that the market demand will be 29,980,000 units in 2018 (Market demand = total sales - exports + imports). The growth rate will be 3% y/y, with 3% for passenger vehicles and 1% for commercial vehicles.

China sales increase 0.7% y/y to 2.958 million units in November
On December 11, 2017 the China Association of Automobile Manufactures (CAAM) announced vehicle production and sales results for November 2017. Production and sales showed increases on both a month-over-month (m/m) basis and year-over-year (y/y) basis. Y/y growth of production and sales for January to November stayed at 3% to 4%. Passenger vehicle production and sales saw growth of around 2%, a lower y/y growth compared to total vehicle data, while commercial vehicles maintained growth of around 15%, higher y/y than total vehicle data.
Overall vehicle production in November totaled 3.08 million units, up 2.3% y/y, while sales reached 2.958 million units, up 0.7% y/y. YTD production for the period January to November totaled 25.999 million units, up 3.9% y/y, and YTD sales totaled 25.845 million units, up 3.6% y/y.
Passenger vehicle production in November was 2.669 million units, a 1.2% increase y/y, while sales were 2.589 million units, basically the same compared to last year. Sales of passenger vehicles with engines that were 1.6L or lower declined 1.3% y/y to 1.801 million units, which accounted for 69.6% of all passenger vehicles sold.
In the commercial vehicle market, production totaled 411,000 units, up 10.4% y/y. Sales reached 368,000 units, up 5.6% y/y.
As for new energy vehicles (NEVs), production reached 122,000 units, up 70.1% y/y, and sales totaled 119,000 units, up 83.0% y/y. Among NEVs, EV production reached 105,000 units, up 64.3% y/y, while sales reached 102,000 units, up 74.9% y/y. Plug-in hybrid vehicle (PHV) production totaled 17,000 units, for a y/y increase of 117.0%, and PHV sales totaled 17,000 units for a y/y increase of 154.0%.
Exports for November reached 88,000 units, up 18.1% y/y. YTD exports total 797,000 units, up 26.6% y/y ( a 2.3% decline compared to that of January to November). This figure included 564,000 passenger vehicles (up 34.6% y/y) and 232,000 commercial vehicles (up 10.5% y/y).

China sales increase 5.7% y/y to 2.71 million units in September
On October 12, 2017, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced vehicle production and sales results for September 2017. Both production and sales were up on an m/m and y/y basis, with significant rises on m/m. Growth rates of production and sales for Jan-Sep period continued to remain in the range of 4-5%. Among them, the growth rates of passenger vehicles remain lower at about 3% y/y while commercial vehicle maintained healthy at 16-18%.
Production for September increased by 27.6% m/m, and 5.5% y/y to 2.671 million units, while sales rose by 23.9% m/m and 5.7% y/y to 2.709 million units.

China sales increase 0.2% y/y to 2.52 million vehicles in January
On February 13, 2017, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced vehicle production and sales results for January 2017. Production and sales significantly declined on a month-over-month (m/m) basis, while production slightly fell and sales edged up on a year-over-year (y/y) basis. Production and sales were affected by fewer working days due to the earlier start of the Lunar New Year holidays, as well as a lower tax reduction for small vehicles. While a 10% tax is levied on a new vehicle purchase, the tax rate for small vehicles with 1.6L or smaller engines were reduced to 5% until the end of 2016. Thereafter, in January 2017, the small car tax rate was increased to 7.5%.
Overall vehicle production totaled 2.369 million units, down 3.9% y/y, while sales reached 2.52 million units, up 0.2% y/y. Passenger car production was 2.072 million units, a 4.2% decrease y/y, while passenger car sales totaled 2.218 million units, a 1.1% decrease y/y. Sales of small vehicles with 1.6-liter or smaller engines increased 1.3% y/y to 1.588 million units, which accounted for 71.6% of all passenger vehicles sold. In the commercial vehicle market, production totaled approximately 297,000 units, down 1.6% y/y, while sales reached approximately 301,000 units, up 11.3% y/y.
Production of new energy vehicles totaled 6,889 units, down 69.1% y/y, and sales of these vehicles totaled 5,682 units, down 74.4% y/y. By vehicle type, electric vehicle (EV) production was 5,857 units for a y/y decrease of 63.8%, and EV sales totaled 4,978 units for y/y decline of 67.8%. Plug-in hybrid vehicle (PHV) production totaled 1,032 units for a y/y decrease of 83.2%, and PHV sales totaled 704 units for an 89.5% fall y/y.
Vehicle exports in January totaled approximately 58,000 units, a 26% decrease m/m. Exports maintained y/y growth for a gain of 31%. By vehicle type, 43,000 passenger vehicles were exported, which was a decrease of 26% m/m and an increase of 53.5% y/y. Exports of commercial vehicles totaled approximately 16,000 units, a 26% decrease m/m and a 6.6% decline y/y.

January sales in China: Nissan, Toyota, Mazda see y/y decline due to Lunar New Year
In January 2017, Nissan, Toyota, and Mazda saw their sales in China decrease on a year-over-year (y/y) basis, while Honda continued to achieve y/y growth for a marginal increase. Sales were impacted by fewer working days due to the earlier start of the Lunar New Year holidays. The decline was also attributable to a lower tax reduction rate for small vehicles with 1.6L or smaller engines. While a 10% tax is levied on a new vehicle purchase, a lower 5% tax rate was applied to small vehicles with 1.6L or smaller engines until the end of 2016. Thereafter, in January 2017, the small car tax rate was increased to 7.5%.
Nissan sold 119,411 vehicles, which was a 6.2% decrease y/y. Its sales were affected by fewer working days due to the Lunar New Year holidays, and a decrease in demand for small vehicles after strong December sales (before the small car tax rate was increased). Toyota sold 101,800 vehicles, down 18.7% y/y due mainly to slow sales of the Corolla and Levin, the OEMs’ major models. Mazda reported sales of 24,994 units, down 3.7% y/y impacted by the Lunar New Year holidays.
Meanwhile, Honda achieved a y/y rise of 5.3% by selling 113,044 vehicles. The company’s sales were the second highest among Japanese automakers for the second consecutive month. Honda attained record sales for the month of January thanks to strong sales of the Civic and Accord, despite decreases in sales of the Vezel and XR-V, which sold well in 2016.
